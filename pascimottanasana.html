PAŚCIMOTTĀNĀSANA

Stretching the spine forward paścima = west (the back of body) uttāna = stretched out This simple looking posture is one of the most powerful and important of all the postures. It helps to ease the spinal compression caused by standing upright. Continued practice contributes greatly toward keeping the back supple, joints mobile, nervous system invigorated, and internal organs toned. It also helps in preventing diabetes by giving a natural massage to the pancreas. Paścimottānāsana releases the energies in the main nāḍīs (astral tubes) along the spine. It stimulates the maṇipūra cakra.
